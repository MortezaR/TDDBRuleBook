TDDB (Tower Defense Deck Builder) Rulebook

Overview

TDDB is a Tower Defense Deck Builder game that blends the strategic placement of towers with the tactical management of a personal deck of cards. Players improve their decks to generate resources like Gold and Population, which are used to purchase better cards, towers, and send Critters at opponents. The ultimate goal is to overwhelm your opponent's defenses, causing their HP (Health Points) to drop to zero, thus winning the game.

Gameplay Mechanics

Objective

  • Defend your base using towers strategically placed on the board.
  • Enhance your deck to generate more resources and powerful effects.
  • Attack your opponent by sending Critters to breach their defenses.
  • Reduce your opponent's HP to zero to achieve victory.

Currencies

The game features multiple currencies, each serving specific purposes:

Gold (G)

  • Usage: Purchase cards from the Shop.
  • Characteristics:
    • Temporary: Gold resets to zero at the end of each turn.
    • Acquisition: Generated by playing certain cards.
  • Note: Unspent Gold converts to Coffers at a 2G:1C rate at the end of your turn.

Coffers (C)

  • Usage:
    • Purchase Towers.
    • Supplement Gold when buying cards if Gold is insufficient.
  • Characteristics:
    • Persistent: Coffers carry over between turns.
    • Conversion:
      • Buying Towers: If you lack Coffers, Gold can be converted at 2G:1C to make up the difference.
  • Spending Order:
    • Buying Cards: Gold is used first, then Coffers.
    • Buying Towers: Coffers are used first, then Gold.

Population (POP)

  • Usage: Send Critters to attack your opponent.
  • Components:
    • POP Available: Current amount you can spend.
    • POP Income: Amount added to POP Available at the start of each turn.
  • Mechanics:
    • Increasing POP: Play cards that generate POP to increase both Available and Income.
    • Persistent Growth: POP Income never decreases, allowing for escalating attacks.

Scraps

  • Usage:
    • Upgrade your Shop Level.
    • Reroll the Shop for new card options.
  • Acquisition:
    • Sending Critters: Earn Scraps equal to the POP spent.
    • Card Effects: Some cards generate Scraps.
    • Interest: Earned on existing Scraps at a rate of 1 Scrap per 10 Scraps held.
  • Interest Cap: Maximum interest per turn is 10 times your current Shop Level.

Cards

  • Hand Management:
    • Energy Cost: Cards require Energy to play, indicated by the blue number.
    • Free Cards: Some cards cost 0 Energy and can be played without cost.
  • Deck Composition:
    • Drawing Cards: New cards are drawn each turn from your deck.
    • Discard Pile: Played or discarded cards go here and are reshuffled when the deck is empty.
  • Effects:
    • Generate resources (Gold, Coffers, POP, Scraps).
    • Provide bonuses like additional Energy or special abilities.

Critters

  • Purpose: Units sent to breach the opponent's defenses.
  • Cost: POP Available, indicated on the bottom right of the Critter.
  • Acquisition: More powerful Critters become available as you upgrade your Shop Level.
  • Mechanics:
    • Sending Critters: Spend POP Available in the Critters menu.
    • Earning Scraps: Gain Scraps equal to the POP spent on Critters.

Towers

  • Purpose: Defend against incoming Critters.
  • Placement: Strategically position on the board, considering terrain bonuses.
  • Cost: Coffers (C), with the option to convert Gold if necessary.
  • Upgrading:
    • Process: Spend Coffers equal to the Tower's cost.
    • Options: Choose from different upgrade paths unique to each Tower type.
    • Limit: Each Tower can be upgraded only once.

Shop

  • Location: Top of the screen.
  • Features:
    • Buy Cards: Use Gold and Coffers.
    • Upgrade Shop Level: Spend Scraps to access better cards and Critters.
    • Reroll: Spend Scraps to refresh the card selection.
    • Freeze: Preserve the current card selection for the next turn.
  • Shop Level Benefits:
    • Unlocks higher-tier cards and Critters.
    • Increases the number of cards available in future Shop rolls.
    • Raises the cap on Scraps interest earnings.

Board Layout

Interface Overview

  • Top Left:
    • Your Stats:
      • HP (Heart icon)
      • Population (Bird icon)
      • Scraps (Brown icon)
      • Gold (Yellow icon)
      • Coffers (Red icon)
  • Top Right:
    • Opponent's Stats:
      • HP (Heart icon)
      • Population (Snake icon)
  • Bottom Screen:
    • Deck Information:
      • Deck: Remaining cards to draw.
      • Hand: Current cards available to play.
      • Discard Pile: Cards used or discarded this turn.
      • Trashed Cards: Permanently removed from play.
      • Played Cards: Displayed as smaller cards in the top left.
  • Shop Interface:
    • Access: Click the Shop at the top of the screen.
    • Options: Buy cards, upgrade Shop Level, freeze, reroll, and close the Shop.
  • Left Side Menus:
    • Critters Menu: View and send Critters to attack the opponent.
    • Towers Menu: Purchase and place Towers on the board.

The Game Board

  • Terrain Features:
    • Hills: Provide a significant range boost to Towers placed upon them.
    • Trees and Roads: Some Towers receive bonuses or penalties based on adjacency.
  • Path:
    • Critter Route: Critters follow a set path from the spawn point (Arrow) to your base.
    • Defense Strategy: Place Towers along the path to maximize damage to incoming Critters.
  • Waves:
    • Composition:
      • Default Waves: Standard Critters that attack each round.
      • Opponent's Critters: Additional threats sent by your opponent.
    • Information Access:
      • Upcoming Waves: Mouse over "Wave X" at the top to preview.
      • Current Wave: View the Critters in the rectangle at the center of the board.

Turn Structure

Your Turn

  1. Play Cards:
    • Spend Energy to play cards from your hand.
    • Generate resources and trigger effects.
  2. Manage Resources:
    • Gold: Buy new cards.
    • Coffers: Purchase Towers or supplement Gold.
    • POP: Send Critters to attack the opponent.
    • Scraps: Upgrade Shop Level or reroll the Shop.
  3. Buy and Place Towers:
    • Use Coffers to buy Towers from the Towers menu.
    • Place them strategically on the board, considering terrain bonuses.
  4. Send Critters:
    • Spend POP Available in the Critters menu to send Critters.
    • Earn Scraps equal to the POP spent.
  5. Upgrade Towers:
    • Select a Tower and spend Coffers equal to its cost.
    • Choose an upgrade path to enhance its abilities.
  6. End Turn:
    • Unspent Gold converts to Coffers at 2G:1C.
    • Prepare for the Combat Phase.

Combat Phase

  • Activation: Begins after both players have ended their turns.
  • Critter Movement:
    • Critters spawn and traverse the path toward the opponent's base.
    • Includes both default and opponent-sent Critters.
  • Defense:
    • Towers automatically attack incoming Critters.
    • Utilize tower placements and upgrades to maximize defense.
  • Damage Resolution:
    • Leaked Critters: Those that reach the end of the path deal damage to HP.
    • Victory Condition: Reduce the opponent's HP to zero.

Additional Game Concepts

Energy Management

  • Default Energy: Start each turn with 2 Energy.
  • Increasing Energy: Acquire cards or effects that grant additional Energy.
  • Strategic Use: Allocate Energy efficiently to maximize card plays each turn.

Shop Level Advancement

  • Upgrading:
    • Spend Scraps to increase your Shop Level.
    • Each upgrade increases the cost for the next level.
  • Benefits:
    • Access to higher-tier cards and Critters.
    • Adds one additional card to all future Shop rolls.
    • Increases the maximum Scraps interest you can earn per turn.

Currency Conversion and Prioritization

  • Gold and Coffers Interaction:
    • Buying Cards: Prioritizes spending Gold before Coffers.
    • Buying Towers: Prioritizes spending Coffers before Gold (converted at 2G:1C).
  • End-of-Turn Conversion:
    • Unspent Gold converts to Coffers at a 2G:1C rate.

Strategic Tips

  • Early Economy:
    • Focus on building POP Income to send more Critters and earn Scraps.
    • Balance between upgrading the Shop Level and strengthening your deck.
  • Tower Placement:
    • Utilize Hills for range advantages.
    • Consider adjacency bonuses for specific Towers.
  • Deck Optimization:
    • Remove weaker cards by trashing them to improve draw quality.
    • Include cards that synergize with your strategy, whether offensive or defensive.
  • Resource Management:
    • Spend Gold efficiently each turn to avoid waste.
    • Monitor Coffers to ensure you can afford critical Towers and upgrades.

Glossary

  • HP (Health Points): The life total of your base. Lose all HP, and you lose the game.
  • Energy: Resource used to play cards each turn.
  • Gold (G): Temporary currency for buying cards; resets each turn.
  • Coffers (C): Persistent currency for buying Towers and supplementing card purchases.
  • Population (POP): Resource for sending Critters; consists of Available and Income.
  • POP Available: The amount of POP you can spend this turn.
  • POP Income: The amount of POP added to Available at the start of each turn.
  • Scraps: Currency for upgrading the Shop Level and rerolling the Shop; also generates interest.
  • Critters: Units sent to attack the opponent's base.
  • Towers: Defensive structures placed to attack incoming Critters.
  • Shop Level: Determines the quality and quantity of cards and Critters available.
